The Standard Work Week Act

Definitions
a) "Employer" means any person, company, or organization that employs workers.
b) "Employee" means any person who is employed by an employer.
c) "Standard work week" means a period of four days, consisting of no more than 32 hours of work.
Mandate of the Standard Work Week
a) Every employer shall establish a standard work week for their employees.
b) No employee shall be required to work more than the standard work week, unless agreed upon in writing by the employee and employer.
Overtime
a) Any hours worked beyond the standard work week shall be considered overtime.
b) Employers shall compensate employees at a rate of no less than 1.5 times their regular hourly wage for any overtime hours worked.
Exceptions
a) The Secretary of State may, by regulations, provide for exceptions to the standard work week for specific industries or occupations where a four-day work week is not feasible due to the nature of the work.
b) Such regulations shall be subject to the negative procedure.
Enforcement
a) The local authority shall be responsible for enforcing the provisions of this Act.
b) Any employer found to be in violation of this Act shall be subject to a fine not exceeding level 5 on the standard scale.
Fixed Penalty Notices
a) The Secretary of State may, by regulations, provide for the giving of a fixed penalty notice in respect of an offence under this Act.
b) Such regulations shall be subject to the negative procedure.
c) A fixed penalty notice shall be given by the local authority.
d) Monies received by local authorities pursuant to such notices shall be paid into the Consolidated Fund.
Commencement
a) This Act shall come into force on [insert date].

This draft law mandates a standard four-day work week, not exceeding 32 hours, for all employees in England. It includes provisions for overtime pay, exceptions for specific industries, enforcement by local authorities, and the power for the Secretary of State to make regulations for fixed penalty notices. The regulations are subject to the negative procedure, and any monies received from fixed penalty notices are to be paid into the Consolidated Fund.
